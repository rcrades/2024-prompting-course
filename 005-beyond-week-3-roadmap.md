# What Comes Next: Beyond Prompting 101

You finished the course. You can prompt effectively, build apps with v0, iterate with a local coding agent, and deploy to Vercel. That's a real skill set — but it's also a launchpad.

## The Full Progression

During this course, you moved through three levels of working with AI:

| Level | What You Did | Tools You Used |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1. **You talked to AI** | Asked questions, got answers, learned to ask better | ChatGPT, Claude |
| 2. **You prompted AI to create** | Described what you wanted, AI built it | v0 |
| 3. **AI worked on your code** | An agent read your project and iterated alongside you | Cursor |

There are two more levels — and they're where things get really interesting.

| Level | What Happens | Tools |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| 4. **AI works on your behalf** | You set up agents that do tasks for you | OpenClaw, custom agents |
| 5. **AI works inside your apps** | You build AI features into apps that serve other people | AI SDK, ChatSDK, Vercel |

Here's what each of those looks like.

## Expanding Level 3: More Local Coding Agents

In this course, you used Cursor — but it's just one example of a **local coding agent**. Tools like **Claude Code** and **OpenAI Codex** do the same thing: they read your project files, understand your codebase, and make changes alongside you.

Understanding the category — not just one product — means you can pick the right tool for the job and aren't locked in.

**Go deeper**: [203 - Local Agents Guide](203-local-agents-guide.md)

## The Terminal: Where Many Agents Live

You've mostly been clicking buttons and using visual interfaces. But many of the most powerful AI agents — including Claude Code and Codex — live in the **terminal** (that text-based interface where you type commands).

The terminal is having a moment. AI tools are making it more accessible than ever, and a new wave of "Terminal UIs" combine the speed of text commands with visual elements. Understanding the basics opens up a whole new category of tools.

**Go deeper**: [204 - Terminal UIs and CLIs](204-terminal-uis-and-clis.md)

## Level 4: Personal Agents — AI That Works for You

What if you could set up an AI agent that does things *for you* — checks your email, organizes your files, monitors a website, or automates repetitive tasks?

Tools like **OpenClaw** make it possible to create agents that run on your behalf without needing to be a software engineer. This is where your prompting skills become a genuine superpower — you're not just asking AI questions, you're giving it jobs to do.

**Go deeper**: [205 - Personal Agents](205-personal-agents.md)

## Level 5: Production Agents — AI Built Into Real Apps

This is the professional frontier. Companies are building AI agents directly into their products — chatbots that actually resolve issues, assistants that help users accomplish tasks, AI features that make apps smarter.

Tools like Vercel's **AI SDK** and **ChatSDK** let you build these kinds of agents into the same Next.js apps you've been creating in this course. The prompting skills you've developed are exactly what's needed to design how these agents behave.

**Go deeper**: [206 - Production Agents](206-production-agents.md)

## The Throughline

At every level — from talking to a chatbot to building AI into a product — the quality of your prompts determines the quality of your results.

That's why this course is called Prompting 101. It's the foundation for all of it.
