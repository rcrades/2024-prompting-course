# Personal Agents: AI That Works on Your Behalf

> **Status**: Outline — full content coming soon

## The Big Idea

So far, you've been *talking to* AI — asking questions, requesting code, iterating on designs. But what if AI could *do things for you* without you being there? That's a personal agent.

A personal agent is an AI that you set up to perform tasks on your behalf — monitor something, organize information, automate a workflow, respond to events. You define what it should do (using your prompting skills), and it goes and does it.

## Why This Matters for Prompters

Building a personal agent is the ultimate test of your prompting skills. You're not just asking for a one-time answer — you're writing instructions that the AI will follow repeatedly, on its own. The clearer and more specific your prompt, the better your agent performs.

## What Can Personal Agents Do?

Some examples:
- Summarize your emails every morning
- Monitor a website and alert you when something changes
- Organize files into folders based on rules you define
- Draft responses to common questions
- Track prices on products you're watching
- Compile a daily briefing from sources you care about

## The Prompting Connection

When you build a personal agent, you're essentially writing a really detailed prompt that runs on a loop. Everything you learned in this course applies:

- **Be specific**: "Check my inbox for emails from clients" is better than "check my email"
- **Give context**: "I'm a freelance designer and client emails usually have 'project' or 'revision' in the subject"
- **Define the output**: "Send me a Slack message with the sender, subject, and a one-line summary"
